HONOLULU’S MAJOR ART MUSEUMS MERGE
 

The boards of the Honolulu Academy of Arts and The Contemporary Museum approved integration this week, press invited to May 2 press conference

 

HONOLULU, HAWAII—In a move that hails a brand new era for the arts in Hawai‘i, the Honolulu Academy of Arts and The Contemporary Museum announced today that the boards of both museums have approved an agreement to integrate the two institutions. The agreement will become legally binding effective July 1, 2011. In addition, the museum welcomes its new director, Stephan Jost, on May 2.

The merger follows months of research and careful consideration by the boards of both organizations. It is supported by staff of both institutions and has been endorsed by area leaders and funders.

“The boards of both museums have shown foresight in orchestrating the integration of the Honolulu Academy of Arts and The Contemporary Museum,” says Jost, who comes to Honolulu from Vermont's Shelburne Museum. “There are so many things that are good about the partnership. Joining forces allows us to better serve our public, support all parts of our campus and create one collection. At the end of the day this is not about the Academy or TCM but about the people who visit us. Other museums will merge in the next several years, I want us to be the case study of how to do it right.”

The logical strategy unites the resources and innovation of the two institutions into a single world-class art museum to better serve its diverse community, ensure financial stability for future generations, and increase capacity to present innovative and engaging programs and exhibitions. It also brings a family story full circle—The Contemporary Museum’s gallery was built as the house for Honolulu Academy of Arts founder Anna Rice Cooke, after she donated her Beretania Street residence to establish the Academy.

Under the agreement, The Contemporary Museum’s collection and assets are a gift to the Honolulu Academy of Arts. The Contemporary Museum's executive director Allison Wong will become deputy director of the merged museum. TCM curators James Jensen and Inger Tully will be curators of the new Contemporary Art Department. TCM head of finance John Talkington will be head of finance of the integrated museum. The rest of TCM's staff will also remain in place.

The 84-year-old Honolulu Academy of Arts has an encyclopedic collection that spans 5,000 years and represents the major cultures of Hawai‘i. It has Asian and Western masterpieces by such art world icons as Vincent van Gogh, Claude Monet, Katsushika Hokusai, and Robert Rauschenberg . Founded in 1988, The Contemporary Museum is the state’s only museum dedicated to contemporary art, with a collection of 3,500 works that begins in 1940 and runs through the present, and that includes works by such artists as Frank Stella and Kara Walker.
