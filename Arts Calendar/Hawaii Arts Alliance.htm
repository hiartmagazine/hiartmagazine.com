Dear Arts Community,
 

The Hawai`i Community Foundation is establishing a leadership development program for senior staff members in the non-profit sector.  HCF is launching HELP - the Hawaii Emerging Leaders Program.   The Program will be led by Bill Coy of LaPiana and Liza Culick (formerly with LaPiana).  Based on the success of PONO, HELP will strengthen the leadership and management skills of senior managers, for more detail on the curriculum and application please see:

 

http://www.hawaiicommunityfoundation.org/index.php?id=454

To be eligible for HELP, senior managers must:
 

*       Work for a nonprofit w/ a minimum annual budget of $750,000
*       Have worked in the sector for at least 2 years
*       Have substantial supervisory and organizational responsibility
*       Have the support of their Executive Director to participate


 

If you know of individuals good candidates please forward the application to them, or let Clarysse (cnunokawa@hcf-hawaii.org) or Pi`ikea (pmiller@hcf-hawaii.org) know and they will follow-up with you.  Application deadline is June 23, 2010; Program start dates: October 5 & 6.

Since HCF does not have the capacity to run both programs at the same time, HELP will be offered in 2010-2011, and PONO will be offered again in 2011-2012.

MOCA received expected, additional budget cuts
 
Dear Arts Community,

Due to economic conditions the budget proposed to the City Council for MOCA outlined an overall 3% reduction in operational expenses. This reduction also was applied to the following program areas:
  • Cultural Celebrations <less $6,500>  Remaining budget: $33,500
     
  • Collaborative Community Programming <less $3,500>  Remaining budget: $41,500
     
  • Preservation and Restoration Program  <less $2,500>  Remaining budget: $42, 950
Over the last few months your continued support of MOCA has helped to stave off deeper cuts and MOCA wishes to thank all individual artists, organizations and communities who have been advocates for the arts and culture in the City and County of Honolulu. The previous threat to eliminate MOCA has been deferred for now!

Here is where we are as the budget goes through the City Council.

Budget Bill 15, CD1, will be heard on Wednesday, April 21, at 2pm.

Bill 15, CD1, proposes a 1.5% decrease to all departments and/or programs. This equals an additional $6,401 reduction to MOCA's budget. While an additional 1.5% reduction is a large amount for a small agency, MOCA feels that the amount is workable and will help save the entire program.

Streamline Sales and Use Tax Recovery
On March 3 I attended a news conference on the impact of passage of SB 2405 on families and businesses. 
 

The 1969 Hawai'i Use Tax Law collects taxes from people who already owe the state taxes.  Hawai'i's uncollected use tax revenue is projected to grow to $122.5 million on 2012.  According to the National Conference of State Legislatures 2008-2010 State Budget Gaps Estimates and the 2012 Uncollected State Revenues on Out-of-State Transactions, that figure is roughly estimated at 5.9% of Hawai'i's budget gap of $2.093 billion in FY 10.


 

We would like to thank Lisa Maruyama of the Hawai'i Association of Non profit Organizations for reporting on the possible benefits to the non-profit sectors (inclusive of Arts and Culture). We know the non-profit arts sector is experiencing loss of jobs and the reduction or elimination of critical programs that are filling the gap in education and are needed more now than ever, so hope that this legislation will "uncover" important lost revenue.
 

For more information on this and other bills impacting the whole non-profit sector visit the HANO site at http://www.hano-hawaii.org/ to see how you could influence your legislators!

  • SAVE NONPROFIT EXCISE TAX EXEMPTION!
Dear Arts Community,
 
The House Finance Committee heard testimony on HB 2878 (relating to General Excise Tax Exemptions) Wednesday evening but deferred decision-making until Monday, Feb. 22. This bill recommends ending the Hawaii general excise exemption for nonprofit organizations, among others. Nonprofit revenues, with the exception of gifts and donations, would become taxable under the proposed measure.
 
We believe that this bill will have dire implications, especially when donations, grants and contracts are being cut, unemployment is surging and the community is pleading for more nonprofit services.

Let your representatives know what you think!
