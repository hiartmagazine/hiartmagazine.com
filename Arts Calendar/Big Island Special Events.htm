Hawaii Performing Arts Festival’s Sixth Season Coming in July 

When the Hawaii Performing Arts Festival takes the stage in July, it does so with vivid memories of six seasons of classical music on the Big Island.  Memories like…

  • The Leonard Bernstein concert at the Aloha Theater where Bernstein’s actual son Alexander made a surprise appearance.  He happened to be vacationing on the island at the time.
  • The neighborhood yellow Lab who wandered into an opera concert one evening.  He did not howl.
  • First season concerts in which the performers outnumbered the audiences, contrasted with 2009’s opening night concert, which was standing room only

The festival has grown from a fledgling startup with 32 students to last year’s event (the largest ever), when 125 students, artists, teachers, volunteers, staff, parents, friends, and families spent most of July intensely and energetically making music all over Hawaii Island. 

Over 45 young artists from Hawaii have received scholarships, and organizers estimate the festival’s lifelong economic impact to have exceeded $2 million so far.  “We bring tourists to Hawaii Island,” states artistic director Val Underwood.  “We believe that great music is part of a great community.”

The festival continues its long tradition of collaborating with local nonprofits.  This season the festival will present a fundraising concert in partnership with the Hawai’i  Island Humane Society, featuring musical theater and opera works all pertaining to animals.  Local food banks benefit from canned food donations at HPAF concerts.  And this summer the festival offers eight concerts free to the public.

The 2010 Concert Series offers a creative mix of old and new classics, both serious and whimsical.  This July’s “Paris in Paradise” productions have a decidedly French emphasis, including:

  • A version of Bizet’s well known opera Carmen
  • A fantasy vocal work by Maurice Ravel 
  • “Paris by Night”, a musical theater and opera scenes concert.   
  • American composer Samuel Barber’s centennial birthday concert
  • A pop-infused version of the TV show “Glee” performed by young artists attending the festival 
  • An opera for children
  • String concerts directed by Ignace “Iggy” Jang of the former Honolulu Symphony
  • Piano concerts by the Ellen Masaki School of Music

Performing venues are spread out across Hawaii Island including Hawaii Preparatory Academy and St James Church in Waimea, the Four Seasons Resort Hualalai, Mauna Lani Bay Hotel and Bungalows, and the Palace Theater in Hilo.  A complete concert schedule will be released soon.

Underwood continues, “Our students come from all over the world, including Hawaii.  They converge one day, then three days later they are performing amazing concerts together.  We are proud of our growing international reputation, and equally proud to feel at home in this wonderful community, making music with many friends, old and new.  We are honored to present Hawaiian culture classes to our mainland and international students this year.  There is a cross cultural element at work here…students and music lovers in Hawaii get more exposure to classical music, and our festival participants learn to love Hawaiian culture.  It feels good to bring people together this way.”

HPAF offers instruction in the following areas:  Vocal, Strings, Woodwind, Piano, and Jazz.  There are programs for local singers and instrumentalists of all ages.  More information is available at www.hawaiiperformingartsfestival.org.  Concert schedules are subject to change.

Performing on top quality pianos is essential for both students and audiences at the festival.  The official 2010 piano sponsor of the festival is the Ellen Masaki School of Music/Thayer Piano of Honolulu who will ship Charles Walter pianos from Honolulu to the Big Island for use at the 20-day festival.   The pianos are then offered for sale at extraordinarily reasonable prices. Charles Walter pianos are known for their craftsmanship and durability,  and they come with a full 12 year parts and labor guarantee.  The public is requested to contact Genette.freeman@msn.com about buying a piano.

For more information about the Hawaii Performing Arts Festival, please visit www.hawaiiperformingartsfestival.org or call 808-333-7378.  During July, the festival’s office number is 808-881-4024

HPAF is supported by County of Hawai'i Research and Development and the Hawaii Tourism Authority, as well as the Bauer Foundation, the Richard Smart Fund and Kuki'o Community Fund of the Hawai'i Community Foundation, and by individual and business contributors.  To support the work of HPAF, visit hawaiiperformingartsfestival.org to make a tax deductible donation.

"Aloha Saturdays at the East Hawai‘i Cultural Center" A free monthly program featuring performances, exhibits, demonstrations, lectures, workshops and more. East Hawaii Cultural Center, 141 Kalakaua St., Hilo. For information, call 961-5711 or check website: www.ehcc.org.

"The Art of Learning" is a program, sponsored by the Society for South Kona's Education and Arts, that takes the arts to public schools in South Kona. The program brings teaching artists to the classroom to assist with either new lesson plans or to augment regular class curriculum. SKEA matches teachers and artists, assists with curriculum integration, funds the artist's classroom time and any specialized materials needed. The curriculum categories offered are Visual Arts, Music, Drama, Dance, Video Production, and Hawaiian Cultural Arts. For more information, contact: SKEA, P.O. Box 256, Honauanu, HI 96726; Phone: 328-9392; or check website: www.skea.org.

East Hawaii Cultural Center Workshops in Arts and Performance. The center presents workshops and classes in life drawing, painting, slack key, dance, comedy and more. The schedule offers a variety of one-day, weekend workshops, and ongoing classes. East Hawaii Cultural Center, 141 Kalakaua St., Hilo. For information, call 961-5711 or check website: www.ehcc.org.
