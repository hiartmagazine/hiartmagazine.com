Men in Lace
May 20-October 10, 2010
So luxurious, so intricate—looking at a bunch of lace samples might not sound exciting, but when you see the actual works (like the 17th-century lace from Milan pictured above) and find out about the history and painstaking labor that went into these 400-year-old pieces of superfine needlework, your mind will be blown. To put the textiles in context, the exhibition also includes prints of guys in their finery. Visitors can try on ruffs, collars and cuffs from the costume station, and Baroque music sets the tone in Gallery 22.
• Go a level deeper by taking the Tea + Tour of this exhibition May 25, 27 and 30.

Graphic Cabinet 6: William Blake’s Illustrations of the Book of Job
The man who wrote “Tyger! Tyger! Burning bright…” was also a master printer. Come see his masterpiece (all 21 prints!)—Blake's interesting take on the story of Job, which includes the attack of the killer boils.

Ongoing exhibitions

 

Graphic Cabinet 5: Private and Public Devotion in Medieval Europe
Gallery 9 through May 9
Exploration of how medieval illuminated manuscripts and stained glass were used as part of everyday religious practice.

Warriors, Maidens, and Foxes: Kabuki in Japanese Woodblock Prints
Robert F. Lange Foundation Gallery (21) through May 23
This exhibition of prints depicting Kabuki subjects and actors from the 17th through 19th centuries includes works by the Utagawa School print designers Toyokuni, Kunisada, Kuniyoshi, and Hiroshige.

Hina Matsuri: Japanese Dolls from the Tsuji Family Collection
Gallery 20 through June 6
See three sets of dolls from the Edo (1615-1868) and Meiji (1868-1912) periods.

A Year of Charles Bartlett: Bartlett in Java and Ceylon
John Dominis and Patches Damon Holt Gallery (29) through June 6
Concluding the Academy’s year-long celebration of the life and work of Charles Bartlett, this special installation focuses on the artist’s travels in Java and Ceylon. The works reveal how he used his expertise as a colorist and draftsman to record with great sympathy his observations and perceptions, and to capture the essence of the exotic locales.

Living Tradition: The Calligraphy of Chen Wei-Teh
Maurice J. Sullivan Gallery (16) through June 20
The first ever U.S. exhibition of the calligraphy of one of Taiwan’s most renowned calligraphers.

Masterpieces from the Richard Lane Collection
Gallery 14 through June 27
See prime discoveries—Chinese, Japanese and Korean paintings; woodblock prints—from the Richard Lane Collection, which the Academy acquired in 2003.

From Whistler to Warhol: Modernism on Paper
Henry R. Luce Gallery (28) through July 3
In an unprecedented move, the Academy presents a major exhibition of 100 masterworks from its modernist holdings, including a number of unpublished and rarely exhibited examples, as well as a selection of important recent acquisitions (gallery pictured above). Artists in the exhibition include Jean Renoir, Mary Cassatt, Edvard Munch, Paul Gauguin, Georges Braque, Pablo Picasso, Joan Miró, Edward Hopper, Jasper Johns and Frank Stella.
