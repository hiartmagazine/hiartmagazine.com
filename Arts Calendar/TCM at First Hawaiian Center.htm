Hiroki, Setsuko and Miho Morinoue

October 9, 2009 – February 19, 2010 - The Contemporary Museum at First Hawaiian Center

This exhibition features the Morinoue family of artists from the Big Island. Hiroki and Setsuko, and their daughter Miho, create work that is continuously inspired by each other and their island surroundings. The range of work for this exhibition includes prints, sculptures, drawings, paintings, and ceramics.

Ray Yoshida (1930 - 2009)

March 12 through June 18, 2010 - The Contemporary Museum at First Hawaiian Center

This exhibition is a memorial tribute to Kauai-born artist Ray Yoshida, who died in Honolulu in January 2009. The exhibition will comprise work from public and private collections in Hawaii, as well as many works from the artist's estate, many of which have not been exhibited previously. Yoshida, who taught art for four decades at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago, is known for his mysteriously comical, semi-abstract paintings and collages. James Jensen, Deputy Director for Collections and Exhibitions at TCM will curate this presentation.
