Niaulani Nature Walk - Free Forest Tour in Volcano.  This one-hour nature walk travels through a lush portion of an old-growth Hawaiian rain forest on an easy, 1/7-mile loop trail.  The walk introduces individuals, families, and groups to the native plants and birds of Volcano.  Guides focus not only on the biological, ecological, and geological features of the area, but also the cultural uses of flora and fauna by native Hawaiians.  Offered every Monday at Volcano Art Center’s Niaulani Campus, 19-4074 Old Volcano Rd. in Volcano Village.  Free; donations appreciated.  Call (808) 967-8222 or visit www.volcanoartcenter.org.

Flip Out a storytelling performance with Lorraine Godoy, Angie Libadisos and Sandra Claveria.  Through a series of comedic monologues, these Pinay lolas (Filipino grandmothers) in their 60’s tell their life stories, exploring and exploding myths about Filipinos. Niaulani Forest Guide Training.  Come share with visitors and residents alike what makes Niaulani forest such a valuable natural resource.  Volcano Art Center (VAC) seeks individuals willing to devote one day per month to sharing the importance and history of the protected old-growth rain forest during the one hour "Niaulani Nature Walk" held every Monday morning, from 9:30 - 10:30 a.m., along an easy 1/7 mile trail.  Led by botanist Tim Tunison, retired resource manager for Hawai‘i Volcanoes National Park, the three -hour long training session is supplemented by an extensive, illustrated narrative, plant list and information pertaining to the cultural uses of various native plants found at Niaulani Campus.  After training is complete guides are asked to lead one Monday morning walk per month.  Registration is required.  Call (808) 967-8222,  e-mail programs@volcanoartcenter.org. or visit www.volcanoartcenter.org

Fireside Stories in Hawai’i Volcanoes National Park. Learn about the history, culture, and people of Hawai’i in this series of informal talks, sponsored by the Volcano Art Center in conjunction with Hawai’i Volcanoes National Park. This month’s presentation is “Na Mea Ka Lua: Art of the Warrior” with Manny Mattos.  Mattos is a dry land forest conservationist and kalai la‘au (Hawaiian carver), who shares stories about creating traditional weaponry from the wood of various endangered native trees. Volcano Art Center Gallery in Hawai’i Volcanoes National Park. Free; Park entrance fees may apply. Call (808) 967-8222 or visit www.volcanoartcenter.org.
