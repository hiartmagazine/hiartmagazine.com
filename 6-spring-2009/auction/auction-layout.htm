Thirty thousand years ago, our distant ancestors descended by torchlight into caves in Lascaux, France, to render effigies of themselves and their natural world, in personalized form, scale and color. Huddled in the dead end passages in those caves in Lascaux, are the skeletons of those who became lost clambering down into the darkness in search of a canvas – an extraordinary place to explore who they might be as individuals, and who they might become as a people.

Here at our gallery, idspace we’ve created a small cave for the artists of Hawaii to explore what might be. They’ve come for five years and offered us visions of how they see our world, creating environments in which we might reinvent ourselves. Our motivation in creating the space was to experience the spirit and imagination of those whose processes and products mark our place in time.

In our society today, guided as we are by the principle of commodity, art is often measured as a commercial product. And it’s true that we sell art in the same way we sell shoes, entertainment and sex. But while the products of art might be marketable, its motivations and essential importance stand outside economics. The risks artists take, and the sacrifices they make for the opportunity to create and exhibit art, testify to the value they placed on the process.

For this particular exhibition, art at idspace will become a commodity. For the month of June, representative works of those who have chosen to paint on our cave walls, will be offered up as for auction. There will be no holding prices. You, our community, will determine the monetary value of each piece of work, not as judge of the works or the artists’ experiences in making, but to measure and determine the value of art in your lives
