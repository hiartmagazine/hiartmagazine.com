Darrell Orwig
 Waltzing with Time and Place
A Retrospective - Paintings and Drawings     1970 - 2011
 MARCH 12 – APRIL 22, 2011          HUI N0'EAU VISUAL ARTS CENTER          MAKAWAO, MAUI, HAWAII
 

      

by James Rhodes
Darrell Orwig is a Maui icon. His retrospective at the Hui No’eau is a celebration of his decades as an artist and curator in the Hawaii islands. His friendship with Jim Rhodes dates back four decades, to Darrell’s arrival here in May of 1970. So, as they installed the exhibition with coordinator, Marcy Lynn, along with Darrell’s son Stephan, Jim documented the event with camera and comment.
 

 


I don’t pose people unless there is no other way, so I was overjoyed to show up to help Darrell and document his show and opening.
 

By the time I got to Maui, Darrell had the grouping and installation of his work pretty well in place. He had walled in the fireplace to make room for 5 more paintings. Bless her heart, Marcy Lynn helped until the last minute.

The flow of the show was impeccable which was no surprise since Darrell is one of the best in the state when it comes to art installations. A tip of the hat to the Hui crew from Omega to Director Carolyn Kilhour for an almost clairvoyant response to Darrell’s needs. In 35 years I had never seen the old Baldwin property in such good shape.
 

Finally, Steveo put his Dad’s graphic overtop of Clifford’s painting in the entry and some glue gun work in back, and Darrell’s show was good to go.

The opening was the perfect storm, the perfect setting, the perfect show and an endless swirl of old friends – a Gathering of the Tribes.
 

Leis were piled on Darrell as is the Hawaiian custom. Mary had to remove some from time to time so Darrell could breathe and see who he was hugging.
