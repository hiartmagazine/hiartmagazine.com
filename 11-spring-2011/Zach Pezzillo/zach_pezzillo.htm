by Patti Millington 

     
 
I was at my desk late at night, opening envelopes containing entries to our Faces of Hawaii Portrait Competition, when one particular face caught my eye. It was a candid shot of a young boy pouting, head in hands. In the same envelope were two other equally engaging photos of the same boy and his brother. I found the first photo so appealing that, rather than filing it in the stack of other entries I propped it up on my desk where it remained for a couple of weeks while I received the rest of the entries and prepared them for the juror.
 

Then, while organizing the entry forms for the competition, I noticed that the images had come from a 17 year old from Maui named Zach Pezzillo. My curiosity piqued, I wondered, “Just beginners luck?” So I looked on-line for more of Zach’s work and discovered a portfolio of consistently amazing images belying the age of the photographer.

 





From backyard scenes...





 


 




and nature,

                                          to people,



          animals...

 

 

and abstracts, Zach’s evocative choices created captivating images, leading me to wonder,
“Who is this kid?!”



 
From amongst the hundreds of shots from professionals and amateurs around the state submitted for consideration, our juror, Kapulani Landgraf, photography instructor at Kapi'olani Community College, selected two of Zach’s three shots for inclusion in the publication of our third volume of Faces of Hawaii! The third was selected for inclusion in our exhibition at idspace a few weeks later.


 


 


 


The focus of the Faces of Hawaii competition and books has been to create a portrait of the time and place in which we live. For the past three years photographers from across Hawaii have contributed photos and stories of the inhabitants of our islands to this on-going project. I’ve had a chance to get to know many of the participants including Zach and his family, and it is clear that the photographer’s stories can be as compelling as their subject’s.
