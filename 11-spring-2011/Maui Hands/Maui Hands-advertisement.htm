About the Artist

Mary Ann Leigh has productively and happily worn many hats in this life and recently had to ask herself the question, “What happens when you get to a point in your life where you can move to Maui to kick back?

Having studied pottery in college, she often thought about finding the time and place to learn more. Aloha, Maui! With all of the art opportunities, wonderful classes, and fantastic teachers available, she thought, how could hard work and dedication not lead to progress? And it did, rapidly and in a big way. You simply have to see these pieces. Mary Ann loves to create the most mind boggling and difficult pieces she can think of.

She says of her work “Creative exploration brings tremendous joy! I see something magical in the world and wonder how to translate the concept into a ceramic work. Inspiration is found in an ocean with attitude, a posturing bird, the curve of a leaf, the flounce of petals, and the precariousness of rock outcrops. Often the struggle to create is just that. The more complicated and quirky the idea, the more gravity defying, the more complex the techniques to be mastered, the more engineering, the more re-engineering, the making of glazes, the re-making of glazes, and especially, the more puzzling the process, the more I delight in the finished product that results when planning, will, luck, determination, and serendipity all come together. When that kiln door is opened, sometimes, and really only sometimes, there is magic.”

Pictured here are two masks. These are an example of the simple construction that Mary Ann will also be demonstrating during the Makawao Christmas Festival on Saturday, December 10th. Come join the fun!
