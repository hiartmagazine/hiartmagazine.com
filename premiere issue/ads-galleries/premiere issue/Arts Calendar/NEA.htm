NATIONAL ENDOWMENT FOR THE ARTS
 The National Endowment for the Arts is a public agency dedicated to supporting excellence in the arts, both new and established; bringing the arts to all Americans; and providing leadership in arts education. Established by Congress in 1965 as an independent agency of the federal government, the Arts Endowment is the nation's largest annual funder of the arts, bringing great art to all 50 states, including rural areas, inner cities, and military bases.

           Newsletter: December 9, 2010
 
 
 
$26 Million in New Grants
 
We've just announced the latest round of NEA funding: 1,057 grants totaling $26.68 million in the Access to Artistic Excellence, Challenge America, and Literature Fellowships categories to support organizations and writers nationwide.

Four of the grantees are highlighted in NEA Spotlight feature articles. Learn more about the excellent work of the Opera Theatre of Saint Louis, Association of Art Museum Directors
Educational Foundation, On the Boards, and the John
Michael Kohler Arts Center. And visit the Art Works blog for an Art Talk with new NEA Literature Fellow Honorée Fanonne Jeffers.
Speaking of Grants...
 

The FY 12 Literature Fellowships Guidelines for fiction and creative nonfiction are now posted! Grants of $25,000 enable recipients to set aside time for writing, research, travel, and general career advancement. Deadline: March 3, 2011.
New in the NEA Library
 
"Creative Placemaking"--commissioned by the NEA Mayor's Institute--is now available. "Creative Placemaking" takes a look at how partners from public, private, non-profit, and community sectors strategically shape the physical and social character of a neighborhood, town, city, or region around arts and cultural activities. For an overview, please see author Ann Markusen's presentation on the study.

"Live from Your Neighborhood: A National Study of Outdoor Arts Festivals" by our office of Research and Analysis is the first publication of its kind to examine the range and variety of arts festivals in the U.S., the artists they employ, the communities they serve, and the roles they play in our cities, towns, and neighborhoods.﻿             
Welcome to the NEA Alyce Myatt
After the New Year, we'll welcome Alyce Myatt as the new director of the NEA's Media Arts office. In this position she will be a leading voice for the media arts field and will manage grantmaking in film, video, audio, web-based, and other electronic media.
 
NEA Opera Honors Show and Tell
 
Video tributes and interviews with the 2010 NEA Opera Honorees and other opera luminaries are now posted on the website and YouTube.  You can also take in a panel discussion starring 2010 honorees Martina Arroyo, David DiChiera, Philip Glass, and Eve Queler.
We're in iTunes U
 
Lots of our audio and video content is now in the "Beyond Campus" section of iTunes U, the educational repository of iTunes. Preview and download tracks; subscribe to any of the collections so you'll know when new content is added!
 
Additional Opportunities
A great community deserves a great read! Funding guidelines for the 2011-2012 Big Read grants are now available through our partner Arts Midwest. The Big Read provides U.S. communities with grants and comprehensive resources that support their efforts to read and discuss works of literature.

Celebrate the creativity of America's young people! Nominate your favorite youth program for a National Youth Arts and Humanities Program Award.
