MOCHI POUNDING & KADOMATSU MAKING
 Date: December 26 , 2010 (Sunday)
Time: 10:00am - 3:00pm
Admission: $25 (Member), $25 (Non Member)

Come and learn a Japanese tradition of preparing for the New Year!
The Kadomatsu, made of bamboo and pine branches is placed at the entrance to homes to greet the New Year. Mochi is a rice cake delicacy served on New Year's Day to symbolize the many blessings that the family will receive in the New Year.
Guests will make a Kadomatsu for their home, prepare mochi and taste the delicate cakes
while talking story with new and old friends.
Children are welcome to help in the creative process.

CERAMICS: East Meets West- Yixing Teapots

A 3-day workshop with Richard Notkin and Ah Leon

Donkey Mill Art Center Holualoa, Hawaii

Workshop: February 18-20, Fri.- Sun., 9am – 4pm, 2011

Additional open studio time included: 2/22 – 2/25, 10am – 1pm

Cost: $350

Contact: Claire Seastone at DMAC (808) 322-3362 donkeymillartcenter.org

Old friends reunite once again to share their ceramic expertise 'over tea'. Experts in YixingTeapots, the widely collected and accomplished contemporary ceramic artists Ah Leon and Richard Notkin will lead this 3-day ceramic experience. They will review hand-building techniques that have allowed them to transform traditional Yixing teapots into an expressive narrative art form. This synthesis of old and new, of East and West, as well as their acute attention to small detail, and a lifelong commitment to the advancement of ceramics, has gained both artists international recognition. In addition to innovative tea-ware, both artists share an affinity for Chinese Tea.

This workshop is a rare opportunity to participate in a complete tea experience, learning ceramic techniques of building functional representation wares, and appreciating the finer qualities of tea drinking during daily Chinese Tea Ceremonies. We will take an excursion around the island to visit a local tea farm and ceramic studio of Clayton Amemiya in Hilo on Monday, 2/21.

The town of Holualoa on the Big Island is a small coffee village directly up the hill from Kailua-Kona. The Donkey Mill Art Center has an open-air clay studio located amidst green tropical foliage and cool breezes with an ocean view, affording visitors a feel of Old Hawaii.

The Holualoa Foundation for Arts and Culture at the Donkey Mill Art Center is grateful to the Laila Twigg-Smith Art Fund of the the Hawaii Community Foundation for sponsoring the Artist-in-residence Program.
