NABE and Americans for the Arts team up for a scholarship opportunity!

The NABE Foundation, the charitable arm of the National Association for Business Economics is awarding its third annual Americans for the Arts scholarship to encourage the integration of the Arts into the economic education process. Americans for the Arts is the nation's leading nonprofit organization for advancing the arts in America. 

Amount of the Award: $5,000 Award Requirements: The award recipient must come from an economic disadvantaged household, have attended a public school, participated in extracurricular programs including (and/or in addition to) programs supported by the Americans for the Arts (i.e. demonstrate a long term participation in the study of, creation in and/or performance in one or more art forms, including dance, music, theatre, literary, visual/media arts), excelled academically, and formally declared the intent to study and apply economics in their pursuit of higher education and professional career.

Deadline: September 13, 2010.  Application and more info at:

Keynote Speaker - Sir Ken Robinson, Ph.D.
 

 
Sir Ken Robinson, Ph.D. is an internationally recognized leader in the development of creativity, innovation and human resources.  For 12 years, he was professor of education at the University of Warwick in England and is currently professor emeritus.  In 1998, he led a national commission on creativity, education, and the economy for the United Kingdom, bringing together leading business people, scientists, artists, and educators.  His report, All Our Futures: Creativity, Culture, and Education (The Robinson Report), was published to widespread acclaim.  He played a central role in developing a strategy for creative and economic development as part of the peace process in Northern Ireland.  The resulting blueprint for change, "Unlocking Creativity," was adopted by politicians of all parties and by business, education, and cultural leaders across the province.  In 2003, he received a knighthood from Queen Elizabeth II for his services to the arts.  In 2005, he was named as one ofTime/Fortune/CNN's "Principal Voices," a project aimed at bringing together globally-renowned experts to discuss some of the major challenges facing our world today.  His latest book, The Element: How Finding Your Passion Changes Everything (Penguin/Viking 2009), is a New York Times best seller.

Breakout Sessions - Transforming Learning Environments Today for the World of Tomorrow.

Available at the Hilton Hawaiian Village site only.  Following the keynote address, there will be two rounds of breakout sessions featuring schools participating in the Schools of the Future initiative.  Representatives from 6 of the 20 schools will present various best practices from their schools that have resulted from participating in this initiative.
 
The Schools of the Future (SOTF) initiative is funded by the Hawaii Community Foundation and managed in partnership with the Hawaii Association of Independent Schools.  SOTF is a capacity building program designed to encourage and mobilize schools to transform their learning environments and teaching strategies in support of student-centered, project-based learning that is relevant to the needs of living, learning and working as an engaged and knowledgeable citizen of the 21st Century.

Dear Arts Community,
 

The Hawai`i Community Foundation is establishing a leadership development program for senior staff members in the non-profit sector.  HCF is launching HELP - the Hawaii Emerging Leaders Program.   The Program will be led by Bill Coy of LaPiana and Liza Culick (formerly with LaPiana).  Based on the success of PONO, HELP will strengthen the leadership and management skills of senior managers, for more detail on the curriculum and application please see:

 

http://www.hawaiicommunityfoundation.org/index.php?id=454

To be eligible for HELP, senior managers must:
 

*       Work for a nonprofit w/ a minimum annual budget of $750,000
*       Have worked in the sector for at least 2 years
*       Have substantial supervisory and organizational responsibility
*       Have the support of their Executive Director to participate


 

If you know of individuals good candidates please forward the application to them, or let Clarysse (cnunokawa@hcf-hawaii.org) or Pi`ikea (pmiller@hcf-hawaii.org) know and they will follow-up with you.  Application deadline is June 23, 2010; Program start dates: October 5 & 6.

Since HCF does not have the capacity to run both programs at the same time, HELP will be offered in 2010-2011, and PONO will be offered again in 2011-2012.
