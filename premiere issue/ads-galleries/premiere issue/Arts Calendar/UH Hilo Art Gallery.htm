Albert Paley Artist-In-Residence Lectures

Albert Paley, the first metal sculptor to receive the coveted Lifetime Achievement Award from the American Institute of Architects, the AIA's highest award for non-architect will give a public presentation about his work on Wednesday October 13th at the University of Hawaii Hilo Ho'oulu Terrace, UCB 127, and on Thursday October 14th at the Volcano Art Center, Niaulani Campus in Volcano Village.  Both presentations, scheduled to begin at 6:00pm are free and open to the public.

Paley has been invited to create body of graphic works in the Art Department Print Studio from October 11-15 as part of the Visiting Artist Program at the University of Hawaii Hilo.

The lectures and residency are made possible with support from the Hawaii State Foundation on Culture and the Arts, the Hawaii Community Foundation Laila Twigg-Smith Art Fund, The Art Department and Student Activities Council at the University of Hawaii Hilo.

For additional information please contact Professor Michael Marshall: mdmarsha@hawaii.edu
