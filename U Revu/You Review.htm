In an ongoing effort to offer voice to a greater cross-section of the arts in our community, HI Art Magazine has created a new feature which will present current exhibitions from artists around the Hawaiian Islands, presented for your review. We will solicit documentation of select exhibitions to elicit reviews from you, the readers, to publish as a representative sample of relevant, entertaining and informative responses to the voices of our community.

 

The views expressed in Urevue do not represent the opinions or views of HI Art Magazine or its writers.

Please send reviews, and solicitations for representation of exhibitions to the editor@hiartmagazine.com.

DUANE PREBLE

My art comes from an attitude of open perception and response to what excites me. I work in drawing, painting, photography, and sculpture and in modes as diverse as my interests. I enjoy the way works of art and aesthetic sensitivity challenge preconceptions and feed the soul. I believe that through discovery and development of authentic expression we come to know and appreciate ourselves, others, and life in the world.

Much of my work is as a community artist. I use my art experience to address public awareness and decision-making for the betterment of the environment we share.

 
 
 
NOE TANIGAWA

I think of the patterns and swirls as the music, the rhythms that connect the visible and invisible world. It’s all energy.

       

 
RUSSELL SUNABE

August 2009

In the post-post world, after the dust clears, in the vast changed landscape, the wanderer travels. Ever seeking, ever moving, but never finding, never at ease. This is the fate for the intellect, for everything is strange and distant. Where is the intimate, the familiar?

Where is home?
