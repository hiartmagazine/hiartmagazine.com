Arts @ Work for Youth

  

 CLICK ON PHOTOS TO VIEW LARGER IMAGES

 

Master artists serve as professional mentors for youth in rural Hawaii.  These young artists develop creative skills by expressing themselves through a variety of mediums. They also produce saleable products while gaining  workplace preparation experiences.

This exhibition is the end result of an exercise in which the students created self portraits using cameras and iMac photo booth software; creating art while learning computer skills.

The Arts @ Work for Youth summer program was sponsored by Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services in partnership with the University of Hawaii Center on Disability Studies, VSA arts of Hawaii-Pacific and Hanai I Ka Hana.
