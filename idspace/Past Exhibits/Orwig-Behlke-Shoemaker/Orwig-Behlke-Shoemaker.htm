Darrell Orwig is the Godfather of contemporary art on Maui. Following a career in the Navy and active duty in Desert Storm as a Combat Artist, Darrell has been an educator and exhibiting artist in the islands since the seventies. From 1994, he worked for the Maui Arts and Cultural Center, retiring as Gallery Director of Schaefer International Gallery in 2009. Exhibitions he directed include The Golden Tea Room from Japan, centennial exhibits for Korea, and the Philippines, a Rembrandt /Durer intaglio print exhibit, plus he developed the Schaefer Portrait Challenge. In 2007, he was the recipient of Maui County Mayor's Office of Culture and Arts Recognition Award for service to the community.

David Behlke has lived in Hawaii since 1992 and has been director of the Koa Gallery at Kapi'olani Community College since 1994. Prior to his move to the Islands he managed the prestigious Wenger Gallery in Los Angeles and has been involved in higher education for the past twenty-five years. As art maker, Behlke has exhibited in the Honolulu art scene since his arrival, working in a variety of media, drawing, painting in acrylics and oils. He also designs and fabricates leaded stained and etched glass windows for commercial and domestic venues.

John Shoemaker came to art relatively late in life, having his first exhibition in 1992 at age 49. After studying watercolor and interaction of color with Richard Nelson, he began work in the print studio at Hui No'eau on Maui, mentored by Eric Sato. In 1998 the State Foundation on Culture and the Arts made the first of several acquisitions of John's work. In 2003 he began working in encaustics and continues printmaking, creating one of a kind "artist books" utilizing traditional Asian and European binding processes and visual narratives.
