Featuring:

    
 

Chikara Miura, born in Nagano, Japan, was brought up hearing church music. He began performing on the drums in high school and became a professional jazz drummer at age 22. Chikara experimented with different blends of percussion materials in his improvisational performances, currently using stones that he hand-picked in Japan, China, and Korea. One of his performances in a forest in Japan incorporated a frozen waterfall and musical sand. Chikara teaches in Japan and performs in Europe, China, Korea, U.S.A. and Japan. For the idspace performance, Elements, Chikara will be playing on stones from Korea.
 
€  

Junji Ishida, born in Kyoto in 1950, studied classical violin from age seven. Early on he was introduced to American country and traditional folk music, subsequently working as a bluegrass banjo player through high school and university. Later he formed a folk rock band, TEA with three American musicians. Since then he's played with musicians of various genres from country music, to blues, and Indian music, as well as traditional Japanese music. Junji currently focuses on solo improvisation using MIDI Violin with computer, performing mixed-genre collaborations utilizing sound combined with dance, images, even flowers and fragrance. His work is an exploration and discovery of natural synergy.
   
€  

Tsuneo Taniuchi was born in 1953 in Tanabe, Japan now maintaining studios there and in Yugawara, near the Izu Peninsula. After working as a graphic designer in Tokyo and Boston and receiving design awards in the U.S. from the New York and Boston Art Directors Clubs and the National Association of Educational Broadcasters, among others, Taniuchi focused on his fine art. In addition to exhibitions and installations, public and private commissions, books and teaching workshops, he has created outdoor environmental design pieces, signage and sculptures in metal and concrete, as well as six books for children, one of which won a Japan Book Design award. Taniuchi's latest solo exhibitions were held at the Kumanokodo Nakahechi Museum in Wakayama Prefecture, at Gallery 5610 in Aoyama, Tokyo, and at the Paper Voice Gallery in Osaka.
   
€  

Janne Watson studied with iconic American photographers, Wynn Bullock and Imogen Cunningham, and worked as a commercial photographer in the San Francisco Bay area for 35 years. While studying and photographing Aikido, Janne began improvising with the camera. From that process emerged her definitive portfolio and a collaborative book with Sensei Terry Dobson, It's A Lot Like Dancing, An Aikido Journey. Her commitment to personal and social transformation through improvisation and collaboration characterize a career during which her work has been exhibited in galleries, and published in numerous magazines, books and quarterlies.
   
€  

Rhiannon is an innovative vocal artist with a vision of music as a vehicle for transformation and social change. From her stellar collaborations with the all-women's jazz ensemble Alive!, to a cappella ensemble SoVoSo, to Bobby McFerrin and Voicestra, Rhiannon has paved a unique path as an independent artist. Teaching at the Berklee College of Music from 2009 to 2011, she has continued to refine her improvisation work, singing in a cappella partnership with WeBe3, and with instrumental trio Spontaneous. Rhiannon's new book about her life and teaching methods, The Vocal River, will be published in 2013
   
€  

Susumu Sakaguchi was born in Japan at the end of World War ll, migrating to United of America in 1956 at age twelve. He grew up in downtown Los Angeles and began his study of fine arts at Otis Institute of Arts of Arts at age fourteen, and enrolled in California Institute of Arts, Graduate School in 1971, where he studied under such icons as Nam-JunePaik, John Baldessari, Allan Kaprow, and Ravi Shankar. After graduating Cal-Arts, Susumu moved to New York City in 1973, where he worked as assistant to Isamu Nagouchi and established his painting studio. Susumu exhibits internationally and now makes his home in Volcano Hawaii, 2011.
   
€  

Shizuno Nasu was born in Osaka, Japan and began her classical ballet training at age three. She was chosen as an exchange student with the Bolshoi Ballet at age seven and made her debut as a professional dancer at 19. She has won high praise in France, Germany, Italy, Greece, India, South Korea, China, Japan and the U.S. Shizunoâ€™s quest for her own stylistic voice led her to study old Japanese mythological dances, undertaking a multi-year pilgrimage, performing in every prefecture of Japan, and deepening within her the skill and spirit of mai, the dance.

She currently resides in Volcano, on the Island of Hawai'i, where she continues to generate new Spiral Visions and teaches at her Shizuno Nasu Dance Institute, aspiring to bring cultures together through international cultural exchange.
