Albert Paley, blacksmith/sculptor from Rochester, New York presented his work Wednesday, October 13th for the Visiting Artist program at UH-Hilo, and again on Thursday at the Volcano Art Center. Paley is a prolific sculptor in his fifth decade of producing large scale architectural and decorative works, with over sixty major sculptures installed around the world. His work is unmistakable in style and impeccably crafted.
Paley was trained at the Tyler School of Art, taught gold smithing at the Rochester Institute of Technology, and became an accomplished artist blacksmith in the early seventies. His large scale forged gates gradually led him to more and more architectural work. His studio in Rochester employs seventeen craftsmen and has produced sculpture as large as eighty five feet in height.
At his talk, Mr. Paley explained that his work begins with drawings from which a cardboard and paper maquette is produced. This model is used to create a four to six foot scale model in metal, to work out logistic and aesthetic detail. From this finished work the production crew carefully scales up the final, immense installation piece.
