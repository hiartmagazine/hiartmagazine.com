Donkey Mill Shows Wayne Levin

by Michael Shewmaker        


A recent visit to the Donkey Mill Art Center surprised us with a delightful blend of scene and substance. We spent the evening of December 16th at the opening of a terrific new show of Wayne Levin’s photographs. A large crowd turned out for this latest offering in the long chain of exciting events at Holualoa’s Donkey Mill.

Wayne Levin is one of Hawaii’s premier photographers. This new show is a portion of a larger exhibition that was staged at The Contemporary Museum in Honolulu. Entitled Papahanaumokuakea, this impressive body of work represents a small portion of the more than three thousand photographs Wayne produced during a month long research tour with NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration). Papahanaumokuakea Marine National Monument encompasses nearly 140,000 square miles of the Pacific that surrounds and includes the Northwestern Hawaiian Islands. NOAA has invited artists such as Wayne Levin to not only document the sanctuary but involve the public in their mission to preserve this vital natural resource. This exhibition will only be seen outside Oahu at the Donkey Mill and will be up through February 12th.
 
 

 



 
Of course, that is only one of the events happening at this vibrant space. Daily classes are offered here. A partial list includes ceramics, drawing, painting, mixed media, printmaking, silk-screening, shibori, indigo dying, weaving, raku firing, papermaking, origami, lauhala weaving and even chi kung or how to make an ukulele.… Should I stop? The rambling facility spills downslope from the main gallery, printmaking and gathering space at the top, to open air studios and workshops below.

Established as the Holualoa Foundation for Arts and Culture in 1994 by a group of artists and art lovers (including Holualoa mainstays Hiroki and Setsuko Morinoue), they have called this unique former coffee mill home since 2001. Be sure to stop in and see what’s happening at the Donkey Mill.

The Donkey Mill Art Center is located near the artist community of Holualoa along the Mamalahoa Kona Heritage Corridor on the Big Island of Hawaii.
