The Legacy of Land invitational exhibition offers viewers a personal connection to nature, through the work of twenty-four artists from Maui. Their efforts reveal the relationship of observation, perception and ideas in a range of contemporary art. A collaboration between the Maui Arts and Cultural Center and the Maui Coastal Land Trust (MCLT) invited artists to create work based on their exploration of MCLT conservation lands including: Camp One Beach, Hawea Point, May’s Landing, Nu‘u, Ulupalakua Ranch, and Waihe‘e Coastal Dunes & Wetlands Refuge. They were given guided tours of the properties and asked to keep a journal to document their experience during the eight months they had to develop the work. The exhibit design includes sketchbooks and a study wall, inviting viewers to appreciate the artists’ journeys and inspire further investigation within the gallery. Artists made use of traditional and non-traditional materials and were given the option to create installations and larger scale works. There is an extended sustaining experience we take with us as we leave this exhibition that heightens our awareness about the essential preservation of our natural places.   -- Neida Bangerter, curator
